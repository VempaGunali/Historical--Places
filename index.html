HISTORICAL PLACES IN INDIA

TAJ MAHAL

The Taj Mahal is an ivory-white marble mausoleum on the right bank of the Yamuna river in the Indian city of Agra. It was commissioned in 1632 by the Mughal emperor, Shah Jahan, to house the tomb of his favorite wife, Mumtaz Mahal.Taj Mahal has been chosen as the Seven Wonders of the World.Taj Mahal is surrounded by attractive lawns, trees, and beautiful lakes.Its breathtaking beauty and exquisite craftsmanship, coupled with its historical and cultural significance, have earned it a UNESCO World Heritage Site status, attracting millions of visitors each year.

QUTUB MINAR

The Qutub Minar is a minaret and "victory tower" that forms part of the Qutb complex, a UNESCO World Heritage Site in Delhi. It was built in the 12th century and is the tallest brick minaret in the world. Qutub Minar is built using red sandstone and marbles. Qutub Minar comprises of spiral staircase with 379 steps. Qutub Minar is located at the Mehrauli area of Delhi, India.Qutub Minar is a major source of attraction for tourists around the world.Qutub Minar structure is the tower of five storeys with 14.3 m base diameter which gets reduced to 2.7 m at the top.

MYSORE PALACE

The Mysore Palace, also known as the Amba Vilas Palace, is a historical palace situated in the city of Mysore in Karnataka. It is the official residence of the Wadiyar dynasty and is renowned for its architectural beauty and grandeur. This palace is also known as Amba Vilas Mahal.It took about 15 years to build this palace. It is made of gray stones and is decorated with a dome made of pink-colored stones.

RED FORT

The Red Fort is a historic fort in the city of Delhi. It was constructed in the 17th century by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan and served as the main residence of the Mughal emperors. The fort is made of red sandstone, giving it its name. During the 17th century, it was the home and political center of Mughal Emperors. Emperor Shah Jahan commissioned construction of the Red Fort on 12th May 1638, when he decided to shift his capital from Agra to Delhi. Originally red and white, its design is credited to architect Ustad Ahmad Lahori, who also constructed the Taj Mahal. The fort represents the peak in Mughal architecture under Shah Jahan and combines Persianate palace architecture with Indian traditions.

ELLORA CAVES

The Ellora Caves are a group of ancient rock-cut caves in Maharashtra. They were carved out of the Charanandri Hills between the 6th and 10th centuries and showcase a combination of Buddhist, Hindu, and Jain monuments.With intricately carved sculptures depicting deities, mythological scenes, and architectural marvels like the Kailasa Temple, the Ellora Caves stand as a testament to India's rich cultural heritage and serve as a captivating destination for visitors seeking to explore the country's ancient architectural wonders.

AMBER FORT

The Amber Fort, also known as Amer Fort, is located in Jaipur, Rajasthan. It is a magnificent fort built with pale yellow and pink sandstone, and it offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.The fort showcases a blend of Rajput and Mughal architectural styles, with its intricate marble and red sandstone palaces, ornate courtyards, and elaborate frescoes. Visitors to Amber Fort can immerse themselves in the rich history and royal splendor of Rajasthan as they explore its grand halls, enjoy elephant rides, and witness captivating light and sound shows that bring the past to life.
